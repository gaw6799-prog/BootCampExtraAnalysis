# Fabrication Evidence: March 23, 2026

## Trump's Claim
On March 22-23, 2026, Trump posted on Truth Social claiming "productive conversations" with Iran
and that hostilities were being resolved, implying imminent de-escalation of the Iran crisis.

## Iran's Official Denial
The Iranian Foreign Ministry and state media (Tasnim News Agency, Fars News Agency, IRNA)
explicitly denied any negotiations had taken place.

Key quote from Iranian officials: Called Trump's claims "fake news" used to "manipulate the
financial and oil markets."

## Market Impact
- Oil prices dropped 13%+ following Trump's de-escalation claims
- The drop occurred BEFORE Iran's denial was widely reported
- By the time Iran denied the talks, markets had already moved significantly

## Significance
This is the strongest evidence of deliberate market manipulation:
1. A factual claim was made (talks with Iran)
2. The claim moved markets significantly (13% oil drop)
3. The claim was proven false by the counterparty (Iran denied)
4. Iran explicitly accused Trump of market manipulation
5. The timing benefited anyone with short positions in oil
